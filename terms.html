6. Broadcasting Rights & Copyright

Sports events and the factual information within them (scores, player names, match results) are public information and cannot be copyrighted. However, the official broadcast of a sporting event — including its audio commentary, video footage, studio segments, and replays — is protected by copyright and broadcast rights held by leagues, broadcasters, and rights-holders.

UltCast's platform is built on the principle of transformative, non-substitutive commentary. This means:

  • You may watch or listen to the official broadcast while commentating;
  • Your commentary must add new expression — analysis, reaction, humour, personality, or perspective;
  • Your commentary must not reproduce any broadcast audio, stadium noise, PA announcements, or other protected material;
  • Your commentary must not serve as a functional substitute for the official broadcast — that is, a Listener should not be able to follow the full game moment-by-moment through your commentary alone without needing access to the official broadcast.

The Replicate Experience Test: If your audio could be swapped with an official radio broadcast and a listener would have the same understanding of every moment of the game, your commentary is not permitted on UltCast. Continuous, moment-accurate play-by-play that reconstructs the broadcast requires a broadcasting licence and is prohibited on this platform.

UltCast reserves the right to remove any broadcast that it reasonably believes infringes third-party rights. Repeat offenders will be permanently banned from the Platform.

Welcome, Creator

UltCast exists because sports fans deserve more than the same polished, corporate commentary on repeat. You're here to add something new — your voice, your take, your personality. These guidelines exist not to limit you, but to protect you and ensure UltCast remains a sustainable, legal platform for everyone.

1. The Golden Rule — Transformative Commentary

The most important principle on UltCast is this: your commentary must be transformative, not substitutive.

A transformative cast adds something new — your personality, analysis, humour, reactions, or unique perspective. It does not reconstruct the official broadcast. A listener to your cast should need legitimate access to the official broadcast to follow the game. You are their companion, not their broadcaster.

Allowed styles: Analytical breakdowns · Fan reactions · Humorous commentary · Tactical discussion · Historical context · Conversational watch-alongs · Passionate supporter casts · Fantasy sports commentary

The Replicate Experience Test: Ask yourself — if someone listened only to your cast, would they have the same understanding of every moment of the game as if they were listening to official radio commentary? If yes, your cast is not permitted on UltCast. Continuous play-by-play that describes every moment as it happens, from start to finish, requires a broadcasting licence. It is not allowed here.

2. What You Can Do

  • Watch or listen to the official broadcast while you commentate — this is perfectly legal;
  • React to goals, big moments, and key plays as they happen;
  • Discuss tactics, player performance, and team strategy;
  • Share your opinions, predictions, and analysis freely;
  • Be funny, passionate, biased, crass, or conversational — your style is your product;
  • Reference public facts: team names, scores, player names, competition names;
  • Cast from your home, car, pub, or wherever you watch the game.

3. What You Cannot Do

  • No broadcast audio: Do not retransmit any audio from the official broadcast, including commentary, studio segments, crowd noise from the broadcast feed, or PA announcements;
  • No venue audio: If you attend the game in person, do not stream from inside the venue or transmit audio captured at the ground — ticket terms for commercial reporting almost universally prohibit this;
  • No continuous play-by-play: Do not provide a moment-by-moment description of every play throughout the entire game in a way that replicates the official radio experience;
  • No official affiliation claims: Do not use team logos, league logos, or any language that implies your cast is officially endorsed by a league, club, or broadcaster;
  • No defamatory content: Do not make false statements of fact about players, officials, or public figures. Opinion is fine; false factual claims are not;
  • No illegal or harmful content: No content that is unlawful, harassing, discriminatory, sexually explicit, or designed to harm any individual.

4. Advertising & Sponsorship

You may include advertising, sponsorships, or paid promotions in your casts, subject to the following:

  • Any advertising must be clearly disclosed to your audience;
  • It must not imply official affiliation with any league, club, or official broadcaster;
  • It must not use protected sponsor branding or conflict with exclusive sponsorship categories held by the relevant league (commonly: betting, alcohol, telecommunications, apparel);
  • UltCast reserves the right to restrict certain ad categories if required to maintain platform compliance.

5. Tipping & Income

Tips sent to you by Listeners are processed via Stripe. UltCast deducts a platform fee of 20–30% before disbursing the remainder to you. You are responsible for declaring any income you receive through tips for tax purposes in accordance with Australian tax law. UltCast does not provide tax advice.

6. Enforcement

UltCast monitors content through a combination of user reports and active moderation. Violations of these guidelines may result in:

  1. A formal warning and content removal;
  2. Temporary suspension of your broadcasting privileges;
  3. Permanent account ban for serious or repeat violations.

If you receive a takedown request or legal notice relating to your cast, notify us immediately at legal@ultcast.live.

7. Your Responsibility

By broadcasting on UltCast, you take full legal responsibility for your content. You indemnify UltCast against any claim arising from your broadcasts. UltCast provides the platform — you own what you say.

In plain English: Be yourself. Add value. Don't reproduce the broadcast. Don't pretend to be official. Don't defame anyone. Everything else is fair game — that's what UltCast is for.
